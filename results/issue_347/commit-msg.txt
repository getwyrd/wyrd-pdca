Resolve every chunk fragment through one placement helper

Garbage collection, reconstruction, and rebalance each walked a chunk's
fragments out to their holding D-servers by hand, open-coding the same
"index space 0..fragment_count(), every index resolved with the
identity-placement fallback" expansion in three separate places. With no
single definition to hold them honest, the three copies could — and did
— drift apart: the rebalance copy iterated the raw placement vector
instead of resolving it, so a live fragment of a pre-M3 (mixed-era)
chunk on a draining D-server was silently skipped. Commit
04ad01aab4bdeefe29e429f84610abf1b963e1d3 fixed that one copy, but left
the duplication that let the divergence hide in the first place.

Give placement expansion a single home. ChunkRef::fragments() yields the
whole 0..fragment_count() index space as (fragment index, holding
D-server) pairs, resolving each index through the existing placed_dserver
primitive so it cannot disagree with the read path by construction.
Garbage collection, reconstruction, and rebalance now draw their
expansion from it instead of each maintaining their own walk. No
behaviour changes — this only removes the room for a future copy to
diverge unnoticed.
