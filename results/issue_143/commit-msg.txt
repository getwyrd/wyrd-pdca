custodian: scrub stored fragments and enqueue bit rot for repair

The custodian had no proactive scrub: nothing walked a D server's stored
fragments to catch bit rot before the data was needed, and a read that
excluded a checksum-failing fragment dropped that finding on the floor.
Detected corruption could be silently absorbed, leaving no durable repair
obligation behind — so a server could hold rot indefinitely with the request
plane reporting healthy.

Add the scrub custodian loop and the shared reconstruction/repair queue it
feeds. Scrub walks each store's referenced fragments, verifies every one's
self-describing checksum against the committed chunk map, excludes any
fragment that fails — bit-flipped or intact-but-misplaced — and enqueues its
chunk for reconstruction. The client read path now lands its read-time
checksum failures on the same queue, so a fragment excluded reactively on
read becomes a repair obligation too. Scrub coverage and the scrub-detected
corruption rate are emitted on the durability-telemetry seam. This slice only
produces obligations; dequeuing and rebuilding remain the reconstruction
custodian.

The loop dispatches through the existing fenced reconcile_step control point
and reuses the committed-reference set and ledger pattern introduced by the
GC custodian in a157aba4cbe985aabd800106bc8dada54b818b0b.
