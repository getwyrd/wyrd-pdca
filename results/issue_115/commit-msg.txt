feat(core): parallel any-k-arrive-first read, cancel the slow m (M2.5, #115)

The fifth step of M2 (proposal 0004): the erasure-coded read stops waiting
on the slowest m fragments and reconstructs from whichever k verify first.

- `core::read_chunk` (`crates/core/src/read.rs`) — the `EcScheme::ReedSolomon`
  arm's in-index-order serial fetch (`for index in 0..(k+m) { get_fragment(..).await }`,
  one fetch outstanding at a time) becomes a `FuturesUnordered` fan-out: fire
  `get_fragment` at all n = k+m indices at once, push each shard that verifies its
  checksum, and break once k are in hand — dropping (cancelling) the outstanding
  fetches. A fragment that is missing (`Ok(None)`), fails its checksum/decode
  (`Err`), or is slow/unreachable (its future has not resolved) is treated as
  absent and read around, so the read waits only on the k fastest valid fragments,
  never on the slow m. Below k it still returns the typed
  `ReadError::InsufficientFragments` — no panic, no short/corrupt read.
  Single-task cooperative concurrency (polls the futures, never spawns), so
  completion ordering is seed-driven and the read stays deterministic under
  simulation (ADR-0009). The `EcScheme::None` arm is unchanged — a single fetch.

`futures-util` (already in the workspace, alloc-only) supplies the
runtime-agnostic `FuturesUnordered` combinator.

Verification:
- DST (`crates/server/tests/dst_read_fanout.rs`) — a seed-reproducible
  `wyrd_testkit::Sim` test in the style of `dst_erasure.rs`. An `ArrivalStore`
  fake gives each fetch a seed-ranked number of poll-yields, so the n fetches
  complete in a seed-driven permutation. Over a 64-seed sweep plus a pinned
  regression seed it asserts: byte-identical rs(6,3) reconstruction from whichever
  k arrive first (order-independent); peak in-flight == n (the discriminator —
  the serial read peaks at 1); exactly k fetches reach the inner store (the slow m
  are cancelled); below k a clean typed `InsufficientFragments`; and `None` stays
  a single fetch. Red on `main` (peak left: 1, right: 9), green on the fix.
- server (`crates/server/tests/read_fanout.rs`) — a complementary over-the-wire
  check: an rs(6,3) GET over real gRPC reconstructs the first k and abandons hung
  D servers; below k surfaces the typed error.
- `cargo xtask ci` (fmt, clippy -D warnings, build, full test suite, deny,
  conformance, madsim DST) green.

`async-trait` enters `crates/server` dev-deps for the in-test `ChunkStore` fake.
