core: reject misplaced-but-intact fragments on the read path

When the store returned a fragment whose self-describing checksum was
valid but whose header named a different chunk, the read path accepted it
on the checksum alone. Such a misrouted fragment was returned directly as
an unencoded chunk's payload, or fed as a survivor shard into the erasure
decoder — handing back another chunk's bytes, or silently reconstructing
corrupt data, with no error to the caller.

Scrub and reconstruction already guard against this: they admit a
fragment only if it decodes cleanly AND its header's chunk_id matches the
chunk being read. The read path was the only consumer trusting the
checksum by itself.

Apply that same chunk_id match at both fragment-acceptance sites in the
read path. An unencoded read whose only fragment is misplaced now fails
instead of returning foreign bytes; an erasure read treats a misplaced
survivor as absent and reads around it (just as it does a missing or
checksum-failing fragment), and the chunk is recorded for repair through
the existing plumbing. The on-disk format and the corrupt-fragment error
contract are unchanged.
