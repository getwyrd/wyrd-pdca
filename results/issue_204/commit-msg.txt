fix(chunkstore-fs): offload blocking disk I/O off the reactor

FsChunkStore implements the async ChunkStore trait but runs synchronous
std::fs syscalls directly in its method bodies. On the d-server's
multi-threaded tokio runtime each storage RPC therefore pins a reactor
worker thread for the full duration of its disk syscall. Under a sustained
burst of writes — or a single large list_fragments walk — the worker
threads stay buried in syscalls, the co-scheduled lease-renew heartbeat
misses its tick, and once the lease TTL lapses the server drops out of
discovery and stops serving.

Route every blocking syscall in the store's async methods (put, get,
list, delete, health) through a small offload helper that hands the work
to tokio's blocking pool, so the reactor — its accept loop, its timers
including the lease heartbeat, and every other in-flight task — stays
schedulable no matter how many storage syscalls are in flight. The store
stays runtime-agnostic: off a tokio runtime (the pollster- and
madsim-driven tests) the work runs inline, unchanged, so no existing test
needed to move.

Also drop the per-write create_dir_all stat on the steady-state path:
put_fragment now attempts the scratch write straight away and creates the
chunk directory only on the genuine first-fragment miss, preserving the
unique-scratch write and atomic-rename publish semantics intact.
