custodian: reconstruct under-replicated chunks from the repair queue

Until now a lost D server (or a scrub/read checksum failure) left affected
chunks under-replicated forever: scrub and the read path enqueued repair
obligations on the shared, durable repair queue, but nothing consumed them,
so redundancy never recovered. The queue was built by
6a33a33ebd7cbcc29b9cc7530832314774ceadbb; this change adds the consumer.

The reconstruction custodian drains that queue each pass. For every affected
chunk it gathers any k surviving fragments, verifies each against its
checksum (a corrupt or misplaced shard is excluded and never decoded),
rebuilds the missing shard(s) scheme-driven from the chunk's per-chunk
EcScheme, and re-places them on healthy D servers in failure domains
distinct from the survivors'. The rebuilt fragments are written before the
metadata change, so a crash mid-repair leaves only collectable garbage,
never a torn or hybrid chunk. The placement record is then repointed with a
single version-conditional commit that also drains the obligation and
orphans the displaced fragment, so readers flip atomically and never error
throughout the repair.

Repair is ordered by a repair-vs-serve priority that rises as redundancy
falls, so a near-floor chunk is reconstructed ahead of a comfortable one,
and the three M3 durability metrics (under-replicated chunk count,
repair-queue depth, time-to-repair) are emitted on the durability seam. The
fleet-wide admission scheduler and the rebalance loop remain out of scope.
