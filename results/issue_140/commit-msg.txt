feat(chunkstore): add list_fragments + delete_fragment to ChunkStore

A D server's stored bytes could be written and read but never enumerated
or reclaimed: the ChunkStore contract was put/get/health only. The M3
maintenance plane needs both ends of that gap — scrub must walk a store
to diff its actual contents against the committed chunk map, and GC must
delete orphaned fragment bytes (today's ledger sweep only removes ledger
entries, never bytes). This adds those two affordances so the custodian
loops in later slices have something to call.

The methods are required on the trait, not defaulted: a default that
silently "doesn't support enumeration" would let a future backend ship
un-walkable while compiling green, quietly weakening an invariant the
whole maintenance plane depends on. They are realised on both backends
(the fs store walks its root/<chunk>/<index>.frag layout; the gRPC store
gains matching rpcs) and the fan-out, with the D server staying
deliberately dumb — it enumerates and removes the bytes it is told to,
making no placement or reference-safety judgement.

delete_fragment is idempotent: deleting a fragment the store does not
hold succeeds, so a retried or raced GC reclaim never errors. The proto
evolution is purely additive — new ListFragments / DeleteFragment rpcs
and their messages, no existing field or rpc repurposed — so a
one-version gap still interoperates (ADR-0002 wire rule). On-disk format
and put/get/health behaviour are unchanged.

Implements accepted proposal 0005 (Milestone 3 — custodians), step 2.

Fixes #140
